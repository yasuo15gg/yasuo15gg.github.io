Japan-Vietnam Space Workshop

Date: One day in second week of March 2026 (March 9-13, Tentative)
Venue: Vietnam National Space Center in Hoa Lac
※ After Tết Nguyên Đán (Lunar New Year) ending February 21, 2026

Background

As part of the growing cooperation in the space industry between Japan and Vietnam, discussions have been ongoing around the LOTUSat-1 satellite project. In parallel, the Vietnam has expressed interest in exploring the development of a second satellite. In response, Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI), GoJ proposed a public-private workshop aimed at promoting further collaboration of both countries. The Vietnamese side welcomed this idea, and it was agreed to hold the workshop at the Vietnam National Space Center (VNSC).

Objectives

• Building upon the long-standing friendship and multifaceted cooperation between Japan and Vietnam, strengthen bilateral ties in the space domain to contribute to a Free and Open Indo-Pacific (FOIP).

• Promote collaboration between the governments and space agencies of both countries in utilizing space technologies for environmental monitoring, disaster management, agriculture, and other societal applications.

• Reflecting the progress of Vietnam’s satellite development and space infrastructure through Japan-Vietnam cooperation, discuss future avenues for collaboration.

• Expand opportunities for public-private-academic cooperation in space business, especially in response to the growing global space ecosystem and increased demand for satellite data utilization.
